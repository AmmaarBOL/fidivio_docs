# User & Role Management

This section covers how to manage the people and organisations that have access to Fidivio. All management functions are accessed via **Setup & Support → Configuration**, except for Partner Management which is accessed via **Setup & Support → Partners**.

---

## 5.1 Role Management

Roles define what actions a user is permitted to perform within Fidivio. Each role is a named collection of access permissions that can be assigned to one or more users.

Role Management is accessible to eligible users via **Setup & Support → Configuration → Role Management**.

### Viewing Roles and Permissions

The Role Management screen displays all roles that have been created for your organisation. To inspect the permissions attached to any role, click the **eye icon** on that role's row. A panel will open showing the full list of activities that users in that role are permitted to perform.

### Creating a New Role

1. Navigate to **Setup & Support → Configuration → Role Management**.
2. Click **+ Create Role** in the top right corner.
3. Enter a **Role Title** that clearly describes the intended user group (e.g. Finance Analyst, Read-Only Auditor, Program Manager).
4. Review the list of available activities and check the boxes for each activity this role should be permitted to perform. Activities are grouped by module (e.g. Data Input, Configuration, Reports).
5. Click **Create Role** to save. The new role will immediately appear in the Role Management list and become available for assignment in User Management.

<Tip>
Design roles around job functions, not individuals. A well-named role like 'Finance Analyst' is easier to maintain than one named after a specific person, and can be reused as your team grows.
</Tip>

### Editing a Role

1. On the Role Management screen, click the **edit (pen) icon** on the role you wish to update.
2. Modify the Role Title or adjust the selected activity permissions as needed.
3. Click **Save** to apply changes. All users currently assigned to this role will inherit the updated permissions immediately.

<Warning>
Changes to a role's permissions apply retroactively to all users assigned that role. Review the impact carefully before removing permissions from an active role.
</Warning>

## 5.2 User Management

User Management allows eligible administrators to add new platform users, update their details, and manage account security. It is accessible via **Setup & Support → Configuration → User Management**.

The User Management screen lists all users in your organisation, along with their assigned role, email, and account status.

### Adding a New User

1. Navigate to **Setup & Support → Configuration → User Management**.
2. Click **+ Add User** in the top right corner.
3. Complete the required fields: **First Name**, **Last Name**, **Email Address**, and **Role**. The email address will be the user's login credential.
4. Click **Add User** to confirm.

The newly added user will receive an automated email invitation to set up their password and configure their two-factor authentication (2FA) app. They must complete this setup before they can log in.

<Info>
The invitation email is sent from a Fidivio system address. Ask new users to check their spam or junk folder if the email does not arrive within a few minutes.
</Info>

### Editing User Details

1. On the User Management screen, click the **edit (pen) icon** on the user's row.
2. Update the relevant fields — name, email address, or assigned role.
3. Click **Update Details** to save your changes.

<Note>
Changing a user's assigned role takes effect immediately. The user's access permissions will update on their next page load or login.
</Note>

### Resetting a Password or 2FA

1. On the User Management screen, click the **more options (three dots) icon** on the user's row.
2. Select the appropriate option: **Reset Password** or **Reset 2FA**.
3. The user will receive an email with instructions to complete the reset.

Use **Reset 2FA** when a user has changed or lost their authentication device. The user will be prompted to re-register their authenticator app on next login.

### Deactivating a User Account

1. Click the **three-dots icon** on the user's row.
2. Select **Deactivate Account**.
3. Confirm the action when prompted.

Deactivated users cannot log in but their historical activity and data contributions are retained. Accounts can be reactivated by an administrator at any time.
