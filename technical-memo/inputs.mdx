This section discusses all inputs for the Fidivio model.

---

## 5.1.1 Points Issued

Points issued are the number of points (units of currency) that are issued in a period.

---

## 5.1.2 Yield Per Point

The yield per point describes the selling price per point for programs that are run as profit centers.

---

## 5.1.3 Standalone Selling Price (SSP)

The stand-alone selling price is the price at which an entity would sell a promised good or service separately to a customer.

---

## 5.1.4 Breakage Rate

The breakage rate determines the portion of points issued that is expected to be ultimately not redeemed (i.e. "broken").

---

## 5.1.5 Points Redeemed

Points redeemed are the number of points redeemed by members in a certain period. The number only becomes available after closure of the period.

---

## 5.1.6 Cost Per Point Redeemed

The cost per point redeemed expresses the cost of redemptions in single point equivalents.

---

## 5.1.7 Points Expired

Some but not all loyalty programs apply an expiry policy whereby unused points are voided upon meeting certain criteria (typically time- or activity-bound). The points are removed from the members' accounts.

---

## 5.1.8 Non-Currency Revenues

Some loyalty programs do not only generate revenue from the sale of points, but also receive income from other sources.

---

## 5.1.9 Program Running Costs

Rewards are the primary driver of costs for mature loyalty programs. But other categories of costs are relevant as well. They include selling, general and administrative expenses.

---

## 5.1.10 Adjustment to Breakage

As time progresses, program operators develop new insights in how the members interact with the program – which shapes the ultimate redemption rate (and therefore breakage). New insights may lead to revisions in the outlook for the total number of points that will ultimately be redeemed.

---

## 5.1.11 Opening Balance (Existing Program)

In some cases, a historical balance is carried forward into a new set-up. In this case, the existing points must be transferred into the new program.

---

## 5.1.12 Opening Balance (Points to be Redeemed)

Only a subset of the points that are carried forward from the old program to the new program are expected to be redeemed.

---

## 5.1.13 Opening Balance: Deferred Revenue

To account for the financial liability associated with the existing points, an amount of money needs to be transferred from the old program to the new program.
